# Voice & Quality Guide

How to make the output sound like *them* and actually be good. Apply this everywhere.

## Truth rules (never break)
- **Quotes are verbatim.** A "quote" card or quote caption uses the person's exact words from the transcript. Never paraphrase into quotation marks. If unsure of exact wording, ask for the transcript or flag it.
- **No making things up.** Don't invent stats, events, or claims the source didn't say.
- **Copyright:** flag before they add music or others' footage to anything they'll post.

## Match their voice
- Use the voice from their brand profile. If it says "warm & welcoming," don't write like a press release.
- Write for the audience they named (e.g., new visitors / local customers), not the insider.
- Respect "always include" and "always avoid" from the profile every time.

## What makes a good caption
- **First line is a hook** — it's the only part shown before "...more." Make it earn the tap.
- Short. One idea. A clear reason to watch/read.
- One soft call to action if it fits ("full episode in comments," "join us Sunday," "link below").
- Use their hashtags/sign-off if the profile asks; otherwise keep hashtags minimal and relevant.

## Photos vs. graphics
- **A real photo usually beats a designed graphic.** If they have a genuine photo from the event/recording, suggest using it with the caption instead of a quote card.
- Quote cards are good when there's no photo and the line is strong on its own.

## Don'ts (cross-platform hygiene)
- No engagement-bait ("like and share if...", "type AMEN", "comment YES"). Platforms penalize it and it signals low effort.
- No daily-posting pressure. A steady 3-5 posts a week beats a daily firehose. Skipping a week is fine; the pattern matters more than the streak.
- Keep it platform-appropriate — what works on LinkedIn isn't what works on TikTok. Ask which channel and tailor.

## When a piece is weak
- If the source has no clearly shareable moment, say so. Offer the best 2 options or suggest a different angle (a useful tip, a behind-the-scenes photo). Don't ship a weak clip just to ship something.
