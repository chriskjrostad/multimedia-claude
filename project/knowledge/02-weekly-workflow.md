# The Workflow — One Piece of Media → Posted Content

Run this when the user brings a piece of long-form media (sermon, podcast episode, webinar,
talk, long video, interview). Default goal: **one strong clip + one quote card + one caption,
scheduled.** Do it as a guided loop, confirming each step before the next.

Use their "My Brand Profile" for voice, platforms, colors, and rules throughout.

---

## Step 1 — Get the transcript
You need the words before you can find the moments. Ask how they have the content, then route:
- **They have a transcript / captions file** → "Great, paste it here or upload it."
- **It's on YouTube** → walk them to the auto-transcript: **1.** open the video, **2.** click the **"..."** or **"Show transcript"** under it, **3.** copy it all, **4.** paste it here.
- **They only have audio/video, no transcript** → tell them the easiest free option for their setup (e.g., upload the audio file right here to this chat and I'll work from it, or use a free transcription site). Keep it to one recommended path, not a menu.

Confirm you have usable text before continuing.

## Step 2 — Find the moment
Read the whole transcript. Pick the **single most shareable 15-30 second moment** — a self-contained, emotionally resonant thought that stands alone without context.
- Give: the **verbatim quote**, the **start and end timestamps**, and one line on *why* it lands.
- **Fallback:** if nothing is clearly strong, say so plainly and offer the **best 2 options** for them to choose from. Don't force a weak clip.
- Quote text must be **exactly** what was said. Never paraphrase into a "quote."

## Step 3 — Write the caption
Write the post caption in their **voice** (from the profile), for their **main platform**:
- Hook in the first line (the part that shows before "...more").
- Keep it tight. Add their usual hashtags / link / sign-off if the profile says so.
- Offer a 1-line variant for a second platform if they use one.

## Step 4 — Make the quote card (optional, only if they want a graphic)
- Give them the **quote text + a short attribution line**, sized for their card.
- Tell them to drop it into their **Canva template** (see `03-tool-guides.md` → Canva). If they have no Canva yet, that guide sets one up in a couple minutes.
- Remind them: **a real photo often beats a graphic.** If they have a good photo from the event, suggest using that with the caption instead.

## Step 5 — Cut the clip
Hand off to the **CapCut guide** in `03-tool-guides.md`. Give the exact start/end timestamps from Step 2 as a numbered checklist. Flag copyright if they want to add music.
Confirm the clip exported before moving on.

## Step 6 — Schedule it
Hand off to the **Meta Business Suite guide** (or the right native scheduler for their platform) in `03-tool-guides.md`. Walk them through scheduling the post + clip with the caption, as a numbered checklist.
Confirm it's scheduled.

## Wrap up
- Recap what's now scheduled (date/time, platform).
- Ask if they want a second piece from the same source or they're done for the week.
- Keep it to this one loop unless they ask for more.
